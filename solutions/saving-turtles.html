Saving Turtles Projects

Conservation Initiatives

Across Ontario, dedicated organizations and volunteers are working to protect turtle populations through various conservation projects. These initiatives focus on habitat protection, reducing road mortality, nest protection, and public education.

Featured Projects

Road Ecopassages

Installing specialized tunnels and fencing to help turtles safely cross roads during nesting season, significantly reducing road mortality.

Impact: These structures have reduced turtle road deaths by up to 90% in some locations.

Learn More

Nest Protection Program

Identifying and protecting turtle nests with predator-proof cages, increasing hatchling survival rates in vulnerable areas.

Impact: Protected nests have shown hatchling success rates of over 80%, compared to less than 10% for unprotected nests.

Learn More

Wetland Restoration

Rehabilitating and creating wetland habitats to provide essential breeding, feeding, and hibernation areas for turtle populations.

Impact: Restored wetlands have supported the recovery of local turtle populations within 3-5 years.

Learn More
