Lake Ontario

Overview

Lake Ontario is the easternmost and smallest of the Great Lakes, spanning the border between Ontario and New York. Its diverse shoreline habitats provide critical ecosystems for several of Ontario's endangered turtle species.

Quick Facts

  • Surface Area: 18,960 kmÂ²
  • Shoreline Length: 1,146 km (Ontario portion)
  • Key Habitats: Coastal wetlands, marshes, bays, river mouths
  • Major Cities: Toronto, Hamilton, Kingston

Turtle Habitats

Lake Ontario's extensive shoreline supports diverse turtle habitats including:

Coastal Wetlands

Extensive marsh systems along the north shore provide ideal feeding and breeding grounds for multiple turtle species.

Protected Bays

Calm, shallow bays offer sheltered environments with abundant vegetation for basking and foraging.

River Mouths

Areas where rivers enter the lake create nutrient-rich environments that support diverse aquatic life.

Conservation Challenges

Heavy urbanization along the north shore has destroyed or fragmented critical turtle habitats. Coastal development continues to threaten remaining wetland areas.

Pollution from agricultural runoff, industrial activities, and urban stormwater affects water quality and turtle health in Lake Ontario ecosystems.

Roads separating nesting areas from aquatic habitats result in high turtle mortality, particularly during nesting season.

Conservation Efforts

Several organizations are working to protect Lake Ontario's turtle populations:

  • Habitat Restoration: Wetland restoration projects in coastal areas
  • Nest Protection: Monitoring and protecting nesting sites
  • Road Mitigation: Installing ecopassages and signage
  • Public Education: Raising awareness about turtle conservation

Learn more about these efforts on our Conservation Projects page.
