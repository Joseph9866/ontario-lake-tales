How You Can Help Protect Ontario's Turtles

Report Turtle Sightings

Your observations are valuable! Report turtle sightings to help researchers track populations and distribution.

  • Note the date, time, and precise location
  • Take photos if possible (from a safe distance)
  • Record the species if you can identify it
  • Note any unusual behavior or concerns

Help Turtles Cross Roads

Spring and summer are nesting seasons when turtles frequently cross roads.

  • Always pull over safely first
  • Help turtles cross in the direction they were heading
  • Never relocate turtles to new areas
  • Wash hands after handling turtles

Report Injured Turtles

If you find an injured turtle:

  • Note exact location (GPS coordinates if possible)
  • Take photos if it's safe to do so
  • Contact wildlife rehabilitation immediately
  • Transport safely in a well-ventilated container if necessary
  • Do not attempt treatment yourself

Protect Turtle Habitat

Help preserve and restore turtle habitats:

  • Support wetland conservation efforts
  • Participate in shoreline cleanups
  • Report habitat destruction
  • Create turtle-friendly spaces on your property
