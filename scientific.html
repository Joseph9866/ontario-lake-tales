Threat Analysis Data

Road Mortality Impact

Nesting Females - 95% at risk
Adult Males - 60% at risk
Juveniles - 30% at risk

Data shows roads pose the greatest threat to nesting females during seasonal movements.

Nest Predation Rates

Raccoons - 90% of predations
Foxes - 40% of predations
Skunks - 25% of predations

Multiple predators often target the same nests, leading to near-total failure.

Habitat Requirements

Research indicates that viable turtle populations require wetland complexes of at least 10 hectares for most species, with Blanding's Turtles requiring larger interconnected systems of 50+ hectares for long-term population stability.

Studies show that optimal turtle habitat includes 3-5 basking sites per hectare, with southern exposure and protection from predators. Basking sites are critical for thermoregulation and digestion.

Data indicates turtles prefer nesting sites with sandy or gravelly soils, good sun exposure, and minimal vegetation. Most species nest within 300 meters of water, though some individuals travel over 1 kilometer.
